aneurysm of the membranous septum .                                       1. aneurysms of the membranous septum are relatively rare lesions     presumably developing on a congenital basis . these aneurysms originate in the left ventricle immediately beneath the aortic valve and bulge    into the right ventricle, the septal leaflet of the tricuspid valve or  into the right atrium .                                                   2. many of these aneurysms do not produce symptoms . others may cause right ventricular outflow tract obstruction or may rupture and result ina septal defect . the resulting shunt will be from the left ventricle   into the right atrium or ventricle .                                      3. an aneurysm of the membranous septum may be an isolated abnormalityor be associated with other congenital cardiac defects, particularly    aortic valvular insufficiency . membranous septal aneurysms may be the  site of bacterial endocarditis or thrombus formation .
