 in any group of poor readers, a large percentage will have ocular,    neurologic, psychiatric or psychologic abnormalities and/or a strong    hereditary tendency . the degree to which reading can be learned dependsupon the ability of the individual to adjust to the psycho-physiologic  needs that are present at the time he reaches /reading age/ in school . if compensating abilities are present, and if the child's reaction is a favorable one, he may read well in spite of unfavorable factors,. if    not, he may become a /poor reader/ . these children should be recognizedin their first year of school .                                           a complete medical work-up should be managed by an ophthalmologist . acomplete ophthalmic examination should first be performed and all       possible visual anomalies should be corrected .
