psychotic reactions of childhood: experiences of a mental retardationpilot project.   clinical team evaluations are presented of a groupof 32 psychotic children who were initially thought to bementally retarded.  emphasis is placed on considerationof as many aspects of the total picture as possible withdiagnosis resulting from a synthesis of the individualpieces of information.  caution is suggested regardinginterpretation of incomplete psychological test findings,especially in regard to prognosis.  the results of thisstudy offer guidelines for differential diagnosis betweenpsychosis resulting from primary emotional disordersand psychosis superimposed on a chronic brain syndromein children.
