The clergyman, Mr. Roper, was present; and his wife, with several other ladies to help, was very busy, pouring out tea and handing plates of bread-and-butter. Mrs. Roper was a kind-hearted little lady, always busy about something. The big meadow belonged to Farmer Grismond, and the annual June school feast had taken place in it for many a year past.
