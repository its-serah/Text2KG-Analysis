Before her lay Seymour Street, where alone an eye of light shone faintly from the nearest lamp post. Through the fog came the warning noise of a lorry picking its way. It was cold, cold, all this, and lonely like an island. Her meditations were disturbed by the maid who brought her hot water. 'My name is Carlotta,' said the girl complacently depositing the can upon the marble topped washstand. 'Yes?'
