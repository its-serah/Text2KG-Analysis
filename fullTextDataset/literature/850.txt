For the most part, Harold ignored his father's occasional moments of tenderness, and spent the larger part of his time with his sister or at the Burns' farm. Mr. and Mrs. Burns saw all that was manly and good in the boy, and they stoutly defended him on all occasions. "The boy is put upon," Mrs. Burns always argued.
