"And they do say—" pursued Mrs. Simmons—but the advent of another customer caused her to break off. "I'll come back to you, Mrs. Davis," she said, with a nod. "Don't you hurry away." And Mary Davis waited patiently, making no protest. A brown-skinned child, in tattered frock and curl-papers, stood gazing about her with curious eyes.
