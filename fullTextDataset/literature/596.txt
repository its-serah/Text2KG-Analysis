As I looked about me at the profound grandeur of space, it seemed to me that I understood why Dorothy had not hurried back to the boat. She had embarked upon the Mayflower, a bride, strange to the ways of[7] men and of marriage, and for sixty-three days the stern-faced Puritans had been her only companions and the rolling sea her entire horizon.
